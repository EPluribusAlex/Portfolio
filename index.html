——— About Me ———

I have always enjoyed the outdoors and favoured the fresh, mountain air, and due to my desire to preserve nature for the joy it continually provides me, I spent much of my high school summers volunteering with the local state park. Having been born in England (which I lived until I was 5), I desired to return to the UK following high school in NC, so I moved to Cardiff, Wales in 2009 where I spent some time studying physics and math.

I soon realized, however, that I needed more time to explore what direction I wanted my career to go in, and so I returned to the States in 2012 where the following year I achieved one of my greatest dreams and thru-hiked the Appalachian Trail which extends over 2,100 miles between Georgia and Maine.


I've had a variety of jobs from community gardens, to outdoor day-camps, to strawberry farms, to bookstores, but it was during a trip to Australia where I spent a year with a close friend that I began to consider pursuing a career in programming.


I have always been fascinated with computers, in particular artificial intelligence, and in the autumn of 2017, I decided to pursue training that would help me break into the world of information technology. Thus I entered the Coding Bootcamp hosted by Trinity and the University of North Carolina of Chapel Hill from which I graduated after 6 months of training in May of 2018. I have found that programming really resonates with me, and I am determined to contribute towards this field while expanding my knowledge as much as possible along the way, learning the tools, methods, and frameworks I need to provide the best possible web experience.
